## What the rule addresses

Commission plans often distinguish an advance from an earned commission and reserve later adjustments for cancellations, funding failures, product reversals, or gross changes. The written earning condition and the transaction history are central to understanding whether a later debit recovers an advance or reaches compensation already earned.

Packs and pooled deductions require the same precision: the label applied by payroll does not answer what the amount represents.

## What to inspect

- Locate the signed plan and the version effective when each transaction occurred.
- Write the earning event, payment date, reversal condition, and chargeback window as a timeline.
- Trace each debit to a specific deal and stated condition.
- Review draw reconciliations, final-pay treatment, and later statement adjustments separately.

The treatment of any debit depends on the plan language, the condition, and the applicable law; this framework does not classify a particular chargeback.
