## What the rule addresses

Time can fall outside payroll when required work happens before a punch, after a shift, between flagged jobs, or through a personal device. Common examples include huddles, OEM training, dispatch waits, CRM follow-up, lot preparation, and closing tasks.

Federal and California standards are not identical. Whether a particular activity counts depends on control, knowledge, the activity itself, and the governing law.

## What to inspect

- Compare punches with system timestamps, training logs, messages, repair orders, and opening or closing checklists.
- Identify policies or incentives that make recorded schedules difficult to follow.
- Review remote and personal-device work separately.
- Preserve evidence of both required activity and opportunities to report time.

A timestamp is a lead for factual review, not by itself a determination that compensable work occurred.
