## What the rule addresses

Output-based pay may not visibly assign compensation to rest periods, waiting, meetings, or other time that produces no flag or sale. California has specific rules for piece-rate rest and nonproductive time, while commission plans can raise a related but distinct rest-pay question.

The classification of the payment method matters: a piece rate, commission, hourly wage, and recoverable advance do not perform the same function.

## What to inspect

- Classify each pay component by how it is actually calculated and recovered.
- Compare rest periods and nonproductive time with the corresponding statement lines and rates.
- Check whether an hourly component is a genuine payment or later reconciled against output.
- Keep statutory rest-pay calculations separate from overtime regular-rate calculations.

These questions require the plan, statements, time records, and transaction history together.
