## What the rule addresses

Break rules address whether meal and rest periods were timely, long enough, and free of the duties the governing rule requires. In dealerships, customer coverage, lane opening, active repairs, and single-person counters can make the records and the working reality diverge.

When a premium is due, the applicable rate can include more than the stated base rate. Related statement and final-pay questions have their own standards.

## What to inspect

- Compare time records with schedules, repair activity, CRM events, and coverage assignments.
- Review automatic meal deductions and any process for reporting interrupted or missed breaks.
- Identify whether employees remained responsible for phones, customers, vehicles, or counters.
- Verify the rate and statement treatment used for any paid premium.

Recorded exceptions should be investigated, not treated as automatic proof of a legal conclusion.
