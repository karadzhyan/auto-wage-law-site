# Release fidelity ledger

Reviewed 2026-07-18 against the five accepted v2 concept images in this directory. The comparison is functional as well as visual: each instrument was exercised in the browser, each responsive surface was inspected at 1536px and 320px, and document width was measured rather than inferred from screenshots.

## Homepage

Concept: `analytical-home-v2.png`

Release evidence: `final-home-1536.png`, `final-home-1280.png`, `final-home-390.png`, `final-home-320.png`

1. **Information thesis:** The final hero preserves the concept's central move from generic wage information to a disciplined analytical method. The proposition is now explicit: map the pay system before evaluating the result.
2. **Structural spine:** The six-stage `Facts -> Classify -> Measure -> Test -> Verify -> Act` sequence is no longer decorative. It recurs in the analysis map, article rails, instruments, evidence system, and authority register.
3. **Role architecture:** The concept's role-first orientation became a five-row role-by-issue crosswalk. Each cell distinguishes a time question, pay method, coverage route, and proof packet; the role filters are keyboard-operable and announce state changes.
4. **Visual system:** The release retains true white, deep ink, cobalt, and restrained coral; oversized editorial type; fine rules; monospaced register labels; and repair-order/ledger cues. It avoids a card-dashboard silhouette.
5. **Decision paths:** Dealer and worker entry paths share the same underlying records but present different operating questions. This implements the concept's two-sided intelligence model without implying different legal rules.
6. **Source boundary:** The release adds a visible source-check line and jurisdictional scope in the footer. This was not prominent in the concept but materially improves the product's authority discipline.
7. **Responsive result:** At 1536, 1280, 390, and 320 CSS pixels, `scrollWidth` equaled `clientWidth`. The six stages and crosswalk remain complete; only their layout changes.

## Pay System Analyzer

Concept: `pay-system-analyzer-v2.png`

Release evidence: `final-pay-system-analyzer-1536.png`, `final-pay-system-analyzer-320.png`

1. **Worksheet/findings split:** The final retains the concept's entered-facts worksheet beside a live analytical findings register, then stacks them in that order on narrow screens.
2. **Method sensitivity:** The release upgrades the concept by making pay-method classification operative. Flat/piece, commission, salary, hourly, and mixed inputs produce different unresolved dimensions instead of flowing through a single generic checklist.
3. **No synthetic verdict:** Results identify open analytical dimensions, record needs, and applicable tests. There is no risk score, compliance grade, liability estimate, or red/green verdict.
4. **Evidence and authority:** Every finding is connected to a record pull and an official authority proposition with its stated limit. This turns the visual worksheet into a bounded reasoning instrument.
5. **State behavior:** Inputs recalculate locally, reset predictably, support printing, and do not use storage, analytics, or network submission. The privacy boundary remains visible above the work surface.
6. **Sequence position:** The analyzer is explicitly Instrument 01 at the classification stage, preventing users from mistaking classification for measurement or legal conclusion.
7. **Responsive result:** Browser review at 1536 and 320 pixels found no document overflow, console warnings, or console errors.

## Wage Scenario Lab

Concept: `wage-scenario-lab-v2.png`

Release evidence: `final-wage-scenario-lab-1536.png`, `final-wage-scenario-lab-320.png`

1. **Sensitivity, not prediction:** The concept's low/base/high model is preserved, but the final calls each value an entered assumption and rejects invalid ordering with the explicit invariant `low <= base <= high`.
2. **Decomposed layers:** Straight time, overtime increment, and separately entered premium hours remain visible as independent rows in every case. A total can always be traced back to its formula.
3. **Calculation boundary:** The release states what the arithmetic cannot decide: covered hours, legally applicable rates, group composition, time period, responsibility, penalties, interest, fees, taxes, offsets, and defenses.
4. **Operational verification:** A tested 12-person, 156-workweek scenario with 1/3/6 entered weekly gaps and a 0.2-hour premium layer reconciled to `$69,451.20`, `$187,387.20`, and `$364,291.20`; the premium layer independently reconciled to `$10,483.20` in all three cases.
5. **Error and focus behavior:** Invalid inputs expose and focus an alert; valid recalculation moves focus to the live output; reset restores the disclosed synthetic defaults.
6. **Intentional deviation:** The final uses progressive field groups and a ruled sensitivity ledger instead of reproducing the concept's denser all-at-once desktop control panel. The calculation model and exclusions are more explicit as a result.
7. **Responsive result:** At 1536 and 320 pixels, `scrollWidth` equaled `clientWidth`; the input register stacks before the output ledger without dropping any layer.

## Commission Transaction Ledger

Concept: `commission-ledger-v2.png`

Release evidence: `final-commission-ledger-1536.png`, `final-commission-ledger-320.png`

1. **Transaction-level trace:** The final preserves the concept's central distinction among earning condition, original credit, payment timing, advance, and later debit. “Chargeback” remains an input label, not an answer.
2. **Four evidence tests:** Classification, earning event, payment timing, and debit basis each show proof present, proof missing, and official authority. The evidence trace updates with the selected row.
3. **Reconciliation:** Base wages, commission credits, draws, other credits, debits, and net-before-tax reconcile independently. Editing a synthetic `$500` credit and `$125` debit updated component totals and net immediately.
4. **Complete local workflow:** Add, select, expand/collapse, edit, filter, reset, and print all work in memory. The release starts with five richer synthetic records rather than the concept's three and labels them as synthetic at every relevant boundary.
5. **Record discipline:** The final adds an explicit record pull spanning the signed plan, deal jacket, funding/cancellation proof, payroll register, statements, and draw reconciliation.
6. **No overclaim:** Reconciliation does not classify the component, decide when wages were earned, select a payday rule, determine an exemption, or reach a legal conclusion.
7. **Responsive result:** At 1536 and 320 pixels, the document does not overflow. The wide transaction table is contained in a labeled scroll region and the editor becomes a single-column form.

## Deep Guide

Concept: `deep-article-v2.png`

Release evidence: `final-deep-article-1536.png`, `final-deep-article-320.png`, `final-commissioned-sales-320.png`

1. **Editorial hierarchy:** The final preserves the concept's large title, question-presented block, source record, analytical position, long-form reading column, evidence rail, authority rail, and on-page navigation.
2. **Uniform reasoning contract:** All 13 guides now use the same eight-section structure: Question presented, Rule architecture, Decision sequence, Evidence map, Worked example, Strategic implications, Analysis limits, and Primary authority.
3. **Authority precision:** Each guide carries dated scope, primary-source links, authority propositions, authority limits, and next-check metadata. The source record expressly disclaims any confidence score or promise against later change.
4. **Depth:** Every guide contains 1,426-1,705 substantive words after a second legal-content audit. The release therefore favors developed reasoning and source-bounded examples over the concept's compressed single-screen density.
5. **Evidence architecture:** The right rail distinguishes what each evidence domain can and cannot establish alone. This prevents a wage statement, time record, plan, or transaction record from being treated as self-proving.
6. **Document semantics:** The regular-rate guide rendered one H1, all eight required H2 sections, valid dated `time` elements, working in-page targets, and no duplicate application IDs.
7. **Responsive result:** The three-column desktop composition becomes a single document order at 320 pixels. Source scope, article, evidence, and authorities remain present; `scrollWidth` equals `clientWidth`. The longest role title, “Commissioned salespeople,” was separately measured at 320 pixels: each word occupies one intact line, with normal wrapping and no document overflow.

## Above-the-fold copy diff

The earlier release's generic positioning was deliberately replaced rather than polished.

| Element | Earlier release | Analytical upgrade |
|---|---|---|
| Brand | `Auto Wage Law` | `Auto Wage Law` |
| Eyebrow | none | `Dealership wage-and-hour analysis` |
| Heading | `Dealership pay, measured against the law.` | `Map the pay system before you judge the result.` |
| Support | `Independent guidance for the people who run dealerships and the people who work in them.` | `A disciplined wage-and-hour review separates the work, the pay rule, the legal test, the records, and the consequence.` |
| Primary action | `Find your pay plan` | `Open the analysis map` |
| Secondary action | `Run a self-audit` | `Start with a dealership role` |

The change is substantive: the earlier copy promised information and a generic audit; the final copy establishes the analytical sequence, postpones judgment until the system is mapped, and routes the user into either the method or a role-specific fact pattern.
